About S.S. Rajamouli

Koduri Srisaila Sri Rajamouli, born on October 10, 1973, is an Indian filmmaker who works primarily in Telugu cinema. Known for his epic action dramas and fantasy genre films, he is regarded as the highest-grossing Indian director of all time and the highest-paid director in Indian cinema.

Personal Life & Background

Early Life

Born in Amareshwara Camp in Raichur district of Karnataka to V. Vijayendra Prasad and Raja Nandini. His parents were ardent devotees of Lord Shiva, and he was named Srisaila Sri Rajamouli after his mother had a dream during a visit to Srisailam pilgrimage site.

Family

Rajamouli married Rama in 2001, who is the sister-in-law of his cousin M.M. Keeravani. Rama has worked as a costume designer for many of his films. He adopted Karthikeya, Rama's son from her previous marriage, and the couple also has an adopted daughter, Mayookha.

Education & Early Interests

Educated in Kovvur till fourth standard, then shifted to Eluru. He was passionate about storytelling from second standard. His grandmother introduced him to epics like Ramayana and Mahabharata, while his father introduced him to Amar Chitra Katha comics, which developed his penchant for larger-than-life characters.

Nickname & Philosophy

Known as "Jakkanna" after the legendary 12th-century sculptor Jakanachari, a moniker coined by actor Rajeev Kanakala in reference to his work ethic and perfectionism. Regarding his religious views, Rajamouli considers himself a follower of Hindu dharma but not in the traditional religious sense.

Contributions to Cinema

Pioneer of Pan-Indian Cinema

Rajamouli is credited with pioneering the pan-Indian film movement with his Baahubali films. This movement involves simultaneously releasing films in multiple Indian languages to appeal to audiences across the country, cutting across linguistic and cultural barriers.

Expanding South Indian Cinema

He has been instrumental in expanding the market of Telugu cinema and South Indian cinema into North India and international markets. His films have broken numerous barriers and stereotypes about regional cinema.

Technical Innovation

His films are noted for their integration of CGI with practical effects. He was among the first in Indian cinema to extensively use visual effects and list a "visual effects producer" in film credits with Magadheera.

Global Recognition

Through films like RRR, he has brought Indian cinema to global platforms, winning international awards and recognition at prestigious ceremonies like the Academy Awards and Golden Globes.

Cultural Impact

His films have had significant cultural impact, from popularizing Rugby in Telugu states through "Sye" to creating a global phenomenon with "Naatu Naatu" dance from RRR.

Mentorship & Industry Development

He has worked with and developed numerous actors, technicians, and artists, contributing to the overall growth of the Indian film industry. His production company Visvamithra Creations has been instrumental in supporting quality cinema.

Recent & Upcoming Movies

Rajamouli continues to push boundaries with exciting upcoming projects that promise to redefine Indian cinema once again.

Rajamouli's historical action saga, created to commemorate the 10th anniversary of Baahubali: The Beginning. This special cut combines both The Beginning (2015) and The Conclusion (2017) into a cohesive 225-minute narrative.

Future Dream Projects

His long-term dream project remains an adaptation of the Indian epic Mahabharata, which he envisions as a 10-part film series requiring 6-10 years to complete. He has also expressed interest in making films on historical rulers like Sri Krishnadevaraya and the Kakatiyas.
